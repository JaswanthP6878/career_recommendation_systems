Jobs in Demand

Sectors actively hiring in Kenya's formal and informal economy

📊 Labour Market Context (2025) Kenya created 882,100 new jobs in 2025 per the KNBS 2026 Economic Survey. The formal economy's largest wage employer is Education (731,300 jobs), followed by Manufacturing (388,564) and Public Administration (375,100). The informal sector still accounts for ~18.1 million workers — roughly 85% of all employment.

Universities in Kenya

Public and private institutions approved by the Commission for University Education (CUE)

📋 Admission System — KUCCPS University placement for government-sponsored students is centralised through the Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS). Students apply via the KUCCPS portal. The minimum entry grade is C+ (plus) in KCSE for degree programmes. As of 2026, there are 42 public universities and over 40 private universities approved by CUE, totalling 83 institutions.

TVETs & Colleges

Technical & Vocational Education and Training institutions — categories, types, and requirements

🏛️ Overview The Kenyan TVET sector is governed by the Technical and Vocational Education and Training Act No. 29 of 2013 and regulated by TVETA (Technical and Vocational Education and Training Authority). There are approximately 2,600 institutions (public and private) across Kenya. Funding for students can come from HELB (Higher Education Loans Board) for approved institutions.

Key Vocabulary

Terms, acronyms, and concepts used in Kenya's education system

8-4-4
8-4-4 System
Kenya's former curriculum structure: 8 years primary, 4 years secondary, 4 years university. Introduced 1985; now being replaced by CBC. KCPE and KCSE were its key exit exams.
CBC
Competency-Based Curriculum
Kenya's new curriculum (launched 2017), structured as 2-6-3-3-3: 2 pre-primary, 6 lower primary, 3 junior secondary, 3 senior secondary, 3 university. Replaces 8-4-4's exam-heavy approach with practical skills assessment.
KCSE
Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education
The national end-of-secondary exam under 8-4-4. Graded A to E on a 12-point scale. Minimum C+ required for university; C for TVET diploma; D+ for craft/artisan programmes. Administered by KNEC.
KCPE
Kenya Certificate of Primary Education
End-of-primary exam under 8-4-4, marked out of 500. The last sitting was November 2023 — now replaced in CBC by the KJSEA (Kenya Junior Secondary Education Assessment).
KJSEA / JSA / SSA
CBC Transition Assessments
Under CBC: Grade 6 Assessment → Junior School Assessment (JSA) at Grade 9 → Senior School Assessment (SSA) at Grade 12. These replace KCPE/KCSE as the primary gateway assessments.
KUCCPS
Kenya Universities & Colleges Central Placement Service
The State Corporation that coordinates government-sponsored placement into universities and TVET institutions. All eligible KCSE candidates apply here. Application fee: KSh 1,500. kuccps.net
CUE
Commission for University Education
The regulator that charters and approves universities in Kenya. Any institution not on the CUE list is illegal and its degrees are unrecognised. cue.or.ke
TVET
Technical & Vocational Education and Training
The umbrella term for all non-university tertiary education focused on practical/trade skills. Includes National Polytechnics, TTIs, TVCs, and VTCs. Regulated by TVETA.
TVETA
Technical & Vocational Education and Training Authority
The government body that registers, accredits, and inspects all TVET institutions. Unlike universities (under CUE), TVET institutions must be TVETA-approved. tveta.go.ke
CBET
Competency-Based Education and Training
The TVET curriculum model used in Kenya (parallel to CBC for schools). Focuses on demonstrating measurable skills/competencies rather than time-based learning. NITA and KNEC assess competencies.
HELB
Higher Education Loans Board
Government body that provides student loans and bursaries for both university and TVET students at approved institutions. Students can apply for up to KSh 60,000–80,000 per year. helb.co.ke
KNEC
Kenya National Examinations Council
The body that designs, administers, and certifies all national examinations — KCSE, KCPE (historical), CBC assessments, and TVET examinations. knec.ac.ke
TSC
Teachers Service Commission
The national employer of all public school teachers in Kenya. TSC registers teachers, handles deployment, and posts vacancies. All public teachers must be TSC-registered. tsc.go.ke
Cluster Points
KUCCPS Cluster Points / Cut-off Points
Each university course has a cluster of relevant KCSE subjects. A weighted score (cluster points) is calculated per applicant — this, not just the mean grade, determines placement. E.g., Engineering clusters weight Math and Sciences heavily.
Harambee School
Harambee / Community Schools
Community-funded secondary schools introduced in the 1990s when the government could not build enough public schools. Less selective than national/provincial schools; less fully government-funded.
National / Extra-County / Sub-County
Secondary School Classifications
Kenya's secondary schools are ranked: National schools (top performers from KCPE, e.g. Alliance, Starehe), Extra-County schools (provincial/county-wide intake), Sub-County/Day schools (local intake). KCPE scores determine which tier a student accesses.
